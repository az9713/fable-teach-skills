# Mission: Juggling a 3-Ball Cascade

## Why
I want to reliably pull off a basic three-ball cascade as a fun party trick — for the satisfaction of "I can juggle" and to show people. Low-pressure; the doing is the point, not performing under a deadline.

## Success looks like
- 10+ consecutive catches of a clean three-ball cascade, on demand, without chasing dropped balls across the room.
- I can start the pattern smoothly from a standing position without a run-up of fumbles.
- I feel relaxed doing it — it looks casual, not frantic.

## Constraints
- Casual pace, practice in short sessions (the research says 15–30 min/day for a few days reaches the first real wins).
- Equipment: I have proper bean bags / juggling balls (don't roll away — ideal for learning).
- Learning preference: low-pressure, build confidence with quick wins before adding difficulty.

## Out of scope (for now)
- 4+ balls, clubs, rings.
- Tricks (Mills Mess, behind-the-back, columns, etc.).
- Performing for an audience under any deadline.
