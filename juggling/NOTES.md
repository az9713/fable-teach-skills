# Teaching Notes

## Learner preferences
- **Low-pressure, confidence-first.** Frame everything as a fun party trick. Quick wins before difficulty. No performance pressure.
- **Has proper bean bags** — no need to cover equipment improvisation.
- Short sessions expected (15–30 min).

## Pedagogy for this topic
- Juggling is **skills-heavy, knowledge-light**. Minimise theory; maximise reps with a tight feedback loop. The lesson HTML should give a self-check the learner can run themselves (count clean reps).
- Teach ONE rung at a time on the standard ladder: **1 ball → 2 balls → 3-ball flash → sustained cascade**. Do not advance a rung until the previous self-check passes.
- The #1 beginner failure mode is **throwing forward and walking to chase** — every lesson should reinforce: throws go UP in an arc, feet planted, ideally facing a wall/bed.

## Progress ladder (check off as self-checks pass)
- [ ] Lesson 0001 — One-ball scoop throw (20 clean alternating throws)
- [ ] Two-ball exchange (the "throw underneath" at the peak)
- [ ] Three-ball flash (3 throws, 3 catches)
- [ ] Sustained cascade (add one throw at a time → 10+ catches = mission win)
