# Resources

Trusted sources used to ground lessons. Never teach from memory alone — cite these.

## Knowledge — Pavarotti's own technique
- **Great Singers on Great Singing** — Jerome Hines (interviews with Pavarotti & others).
  Pavarotti describes: breathe deep, lower the diaphragm, then *remain in the inhalation
  position while singing*; never let air rush out abruptly; keep air compression under the
  cords. The primary-source description of his breath technique.
  - Summary referencing it: https://myscena.org/adrian-rodriguez/pavarotti-genius-forever-entertaining-even-experts/

## Knowledge — Appoggio / breath support (the foundation)
- **VoiceScienceWorks — Appoggio**: http://www.voicescienceworks.org/appoggio.html
- **Voice Science — Appoggio lexicon**: https://www.voicescience.org/lexicon/appoggio/
  (Lamperti 1890 definition; "lean on" the breath; slowing the diaphragm's ascent.)
- **SingWise — Correct Breathing and "Support" for Singing**:
  https://www.singwise.com/articles/correct-breathing-and-support-for-singing
- **Arturo Melocchi Academy — leaning on the breath vs. the larynx**:
  https://www.arturomelocchiacademy.com/blog/the-leaning-on-the-breath-vs-on-the-larynx

## Skills — beginner breathing exercises
- **School of Rock — 6 Easy & Effective Breath Exercises for Singers**:
  https://www.schoolofrock.com/resources/vocals/6-easy-effective-breath-exercises-for-singers
- **The Farinelli exercise** (inhale 4 / hold 4 / exhale 4, extend to 6/8/10) — classic
  inspiratory-hold drill for appoggio. Referenced via the appoggio sources above.

## Wisdom — communities (test skills with real people)
- **r/singing** (Reddit) — large, active, beginner-friendly; people post clips for feedback.
  https://www.reddit.com/r/singing/  *(Use when ready to get real ears on your sound.)*
- A local choir or a few trial lessons with a voice teacher would be the highest-value
  real-world feedback once the foundation is in place. (Budget permitting — optional.)
