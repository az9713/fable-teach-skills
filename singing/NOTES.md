# Notes — teaching preferences & working notes

## Learner profile
- Complete beginner, no vocal training.
- Goal: sing **one iconic tenor aria** (Nessun Dorma / O Sole Mio undecided) proudly,
  for self / friends / family. Inspired by Pavarotti.
- Low-pressure, no deadline. Wants quick tangible wins, confidence-first.

## Hard constraint: VOLUME
- Must keep volume **modest most of the time** (thin walls / people around).
- Prefer exercises that are quiet: breath work, hissing, humming, light "ng", straw
  phonation (SOVT). Save full-voice singing for occasional, brief moments.
- Never design a lesson that requires sustained loud singing as the only option — always
  give a quiet variant.

## Teaching style that fits
- One tightly-scoped win per lesson. Beautiful, reviewable HTML.
- Tight, immediate feedback loops (in-browser timers, self-checks).
- Vocal health first — never cue pushing, straining, or "forcing the high note".
- Cite trusted sources in every lesson (RESOURCES.md).

## Decisions still open
- **Which aria?** Recommend deciding by Lesson 2–3. *O Sole Mio* is gentler/lower and a
  friendlier first target than *Nessun Dorma* (which climaxes on a high B). Lean O Sole Mio
  unless the learner is set on Nessun Dorma.
