# Starting baseline established (complete beginner)

The learner is a **complete beginner** with no prior vocal training, whose mission is to sing
**one iconic tenor aria** (Nessun Dorma / O Sole Mio — undecided) proudly for friends/family,
inspired by Pavarotti. Hard constraint: **practice volume must stay modest** most of the time.

Why it matters: the zone of proximal development starts at the very foundation, and lessons must
favour **quiet** techniques (breath work, hissing, humming, SOVT) before any full-voice singing.
No high notes or *passaggio* work until breath support and basic tone are reliable. See [[MISSION.md]].

**Implications for next sessions**
- Lesson 1 covered *appoggio* breath support (not yet demonstrated — coverage only; wait for evidence
  before recording mastery).
- Aria choice still open; recommendation on record is **O Sole Mio** as a gentler first target than
  Nessun Dorma (which climaxes on a high B). Decide by ~Lesson 2–3.
- Watch for the classic beginner stumbles to confirm/deny next session: chest-only (high) breathing,
  clenching on the hold, dumping air, straining for length over steadiness.
