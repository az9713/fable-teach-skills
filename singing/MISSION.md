# Mission: Sing One Iconic Tenor Aria (Pavarotti-style)

## Why
I want to be able to sing **one famous tenor aria** — something like *Nessun Dorma* or
*O Sole Mio* — with a sound I'm genuinely proud of, to perform for myself, friends, and
family. The dream is "to sing like Pavarotti": that warm, ringing, effortless-sounding
tenor. The doing and the pride in the sound is the point, not a deadline or a stage.

## Success looks like
- I can sing the **opening phrases of one chosen aria** with a steady, supported tone —
  not breathy, not strained, not running out of air mid-phrase.
- The sound feels **free and resonant**, not pushed or shouted.
- I can do it **on demand** for a small friendly audience without my voice cracking from nerves.
- I feel like the foundation is *honest technique* (breath, placement) — Pavarotti's
  "pure Italian school" — rather than imitation or forcing.

## Constraints
- **Complete beginner** — no prior vocal training.
- **Volume must stay modest most of the time** — thin walls / people around. Lessons should
  favour quiet, support-and-breath-based exercises and only occasional full-voice work.
- Casual pace, short practice sessions.
- Learning preference: low-pressure, quick tangible wins that build confidence before
  adding difficulty.

## Reality check (so lessons stay honest)
Pavarotti's sound is a **trained operatic tenor** built over years. We are NOT promising that.
We ARE building the real foundations he used — breath support (*appoggio*), open relaxed
throat, forward resonance, legato — applied to **one** achievable aria. Vocal health first:
never push or strain.

## Out of scope (for now)
- High C heroics, full operatic volume, the *passaggio* "money notes".
- Multiple arias or a full recital.
- Performing under any deadline or pressure.
- Anything that requires sustained loud singing before the foundation is in place.
