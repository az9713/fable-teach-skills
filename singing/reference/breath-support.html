Breath Support — Appoggio

"Stay in the inhale." The engine of the whole sound. Quiet to practise.

Take a deep breath, lower the diaphragm, and remain in the inhalation position while singing — never let the air rush out abruptly. — Pavarotti, paraphrased, Great Singers on Great Singing (J. Hines)

The idea in one line

As you sing out, keep the body posture of breathing in. You resist the collapse, so air leaves slowly and under control — steady pressure under the cords.

Find the breath (low, not high)

1
Tall posture. One hand on belly, one on upper chest.
2
Inhale through the nose — belly hand moves out first, chest hand barely moves.
3
Shoulders stay down and still. No heaving.
Cue: "breathe into your back pockets" · or imagine filling a belt around your lower ribs.

The Farinelli exercise (the daily drill)

Equal counts. The held middle is appoggio — staying in the inhale.

PhaseDoCounts
InhaleLow, calm, belly out4 → 6 → 8
HoldGentle suspension, throat open (don't clench)same
ExhaleThin steady "sss", resist the collapsesame

Start at 4. When 4 feels easy and even, extend to 6, then 8. Steadiness before length.

Do / Don't

Do

  • Breathe low; belly leads
  • Keep the "inhale posture" as you sing out
  • Let air out in a thin, even thread
  • Stop while still steady
  • Stay relaxed — calm, never strained

Don't

  • Raise shoulders / gasp into the chest
  • Clench or squeeze the throat on the hold
  • Dump all the air out at once
  • Chase a big number at the cost of steadiness
  • Push through dizziness — stop and rest

Self-check: is my support working?

  • Steady hiss test — a thin "sss" stays even start to finish (no fade, no wobble).
  • Fades at the end → support collapsed; stop earlier, keep the lean.
  • Wobbles → pressure surging; slower, thinner, calmer thread of air.
