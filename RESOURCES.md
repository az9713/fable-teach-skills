# 3-Ball Cascade Juggling Resources

## Knowledge

- [Library of Juggling — Three Ball Cascade](https://libraryofjuggling.com/Tricks/3balltricks/Cascade.html)
  The most-referenced free juggling instruction site. Clear one-ball → two-ball → three-ball progression, with the canonical beginner warnings ("throw underneath, don't pass horizontally"; bean bags over tennis balls). Use for: authoritative technique at every step, naming of patterns.
- [Troposfera — How to juggle 3 balls, learning the cascade step by step](https://troposfera.xyz/en/blog/how-to-juggle-3-balls/)
  Detailed body-mechanics framing: elbows bent ~90°, palms up, forearm (not wrist, not whole arm) does the throw, two target points ~shoulder width and a little above the head. Use for: posture and the "scoop" motion.
- [JuggleQuip — How to Juggle 3 Balls (for Beginners)](https://www.jugglequip.com/post/how-to-juggle-3-balls)
  Emphasises adding one throw at a time after the "3-ball flash". Use for: the bridge from "flash" to "sustained cascade".

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/juggling](https://reddit.com/r/juggling)
  Active, friendly, beginner-tolerant subreddit. People post "form check" videos and get specific feedback. Use for: when you can do ~5–10 catches but something feels off and you want eyes on your form.
- [International Jugglers' Association (IJA)](https://www.juggle.org/)
  Global body; lists clubs and festivals. Use for: finding a local juggling club if you ever want in-person practice (great for fast feedback, totally optional for a party trick).

## Notes on community
- User's goal is a casual party trick. Communities are offered as optional — don't push them. r/juggling is the lowest-friction option if a form problem stalls progress.
