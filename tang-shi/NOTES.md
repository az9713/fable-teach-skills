# Working Notes — 唐詩 workspace

## Learner preferences
- **All materials in Traditional Chinese (繁體).** Learner reads fluently incl. some classical;
  never simplify characters, never lean on translation as a crutch. English used only for
  meta-discussion of pedagogy.
- Chose **律詩 (8-line regulated verse)** as the composition target over the easier 絕句 —
  deliberately picked the deep end. Respect the ambition; don't water down to quatrains unless
  the learner stalls.
- Mission aspiration ("celebrated for 1,000 years") handled honestly in MISSION.md: train the
  craft, delegate the audition to real communities.

## Teaching notes
- Mandarin is the learner's likely phonological frame — the 入聲 redistribution is therefore the
  #1 predicted stumbling block for 平仄. Lesson 0001 attacks this head-on with 杜甫《春望》
  (dense with 入聲: 國木別月白欲不; plus the reverse trap 勝 read 平 in 不勝, and polyphone 更).
- Notation convention adopted everywhere: **○ = 平, ● = 仄** (◐ for 可平可仄 when line types
  are introduced). Keep consistent across lessons and reference docs.
- Feedback-loop ladder for compositions: (1) self-check against reference sheets →
  (2) 搜韻 律詩校驗 → (3) agent critique on imagery/對仗/用典 → (4) 詩詞吾愛 community critique.
- Planned arc (revise as records accumulate): 平仄 classification → the four line types & 黏對 →
  押韻 and 平水韻 groups → first 五絕 as scaffolding exercise (not the goal, just practice) →
  對仗 → composing a full 五律 → 拗救 → 用典 & imagery → 七律.

## Published page language policy
Published lessons (GitHub Pages) carry English for international visitors.
Your personal learning path stays Traditional Chinese–first; MISSION.md and
in-session teaching remain in 繁體 with no translation crutch.
Implementation: 中文|EN toggle on each HTML lesson; literal gloss (not
literary translation) for the poem, so the characters stay legible as
learning objects.
