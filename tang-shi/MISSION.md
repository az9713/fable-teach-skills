# Mission: Writing 唐詩 — Tang Regulated Verse (律詩)

## Why
Write a 律詩 good enough that a reader steeped in《唐詩三百首》would not flinch — formally
flawless, alive with imagery, worth memorizing and passing on. The stated aspiration is a poem
that is still celebrated centuries from now, like the anthology pieces have been for ~1,200 years.

**Honest framing:** canonization is decided by readers, history, and luck — it cannot be trained.
What *can* be trained is the craft that gave those 300 poems their chance: perfect 格律, genuine
對仗, compressed imagery, and resonant 用典. This workspace trains that, and then puts finished
poems in front of living practitioners — the only audition for posterity that exists.

## Success looks like
- Compose a 五言律詩 that passes 搜韻's 律詩校驗 with **zero** prosody errors (平仄 grid, 押韻 in one 平水韻 rhyme group, 黏/對, no 孤平)
- Write 頷聯/頸聯 (the two middle couplets) whose parallelism a literate reader credits as real craft, not filler
- Explain every tonal and lexical choice in one's own poem — including any 拗救 — the way 喻守真 annotates the anthology
- Read any poem in《唐詩三百首》and mark its tonal grid unaided
- Submit at least one finished 律詩 to a practitioner community (詩詞吾愛) and revise from critique

## Constraints
- Learner reads **Traditional Chinese fluently, with some classical background** — all materials in 繁體中文, no translation crutch needed
- Prosody is judged against **平水韻 (Middle Chinese categories), never modern Mandarin tones** — the 入聲 problem is central, not a footnote
- Target form: 律詩 (start 五律, then 七律); learner chose the deep end deliberately

## Out of scope (for now)
- 古體詩 / 樂府 — the unregulated older forms (different game entirely)
- 詞 and 曲 — later lyric forms with their own metrics
- 吟誦 (chanted recitation) as a performance skill
