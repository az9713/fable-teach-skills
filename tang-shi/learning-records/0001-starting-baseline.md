# Starting baseline: fluent 繁體 reader, some classical, aiming at 律詩

Learner reads Traditional Chinese fluently including some classical Chinese, and chose 律詩
(full 8-line regulated verse) as the composition target rather than starting with quatrains.
This sets the floor high: no character-literacy or basic 文言 scaffolding needed; teaching can
begin directly at Middle Chinese phonology (平仄/入聲), which no amount of modern fluency provides.

**Evidence:** self-reported at workspace creation (answers to setup questions), including the
unprompted specification of *Traditional* characters.

**Implications:** Lessons skip vocabulary/translation entirely and go straight at the three
things modern fluency does not give: (1) 平水韻 tone classes vs Mandarin, (2) the regulated tonal
grid (黏對, 孤平, 拗救), (3) compositional craft (對仗, 用典, 起承轉合). Depth of classical
knowledge is still unprobed — lesson 0001 results will calibrate it.
